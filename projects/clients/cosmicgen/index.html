CosmicGen — colocation & infrastructure architecture.

Brought stability and clarity to CosmicGen’s technical foundation by providing colocation services and establishing an architecture framework that guides how their systems are hosted, connected, and managed.

What I built

A solid footing for systems and growth.

CosmicGen’s technical environment had evolved rapidly, but without a clear hosting and connectivity plan. They needed reliable colocation services and an architecture framework to reduce uncertainty and support future growth. I partnered with their leadership to deliver:

  • Current-state infrastructure assessment and gap analysis
  • Procurement and build‑out of a colocation environment for core workloads
  • Architecture blueprint outlining network topology, segmentation, and redundancy design
  • High‑level framework for hosting decisions and connectivity patterns
  • Operations and continuity runbook to guide ongoing maintenance and growth

Outcome

CosmicGen gained a more stable infrastructure foundation and a clearer model for where and how new systems should be deployed. The colocation environment reduced reliance on aging on‑prem hardware, while the architecture framework provided a shared reference for future decisions. Their technical team now operates with a runbook and diagrams that make planning, troubleshooting, and scaling more disciplined.
