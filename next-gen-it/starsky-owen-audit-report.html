Key Findings

Email and domain controls should be hardened High

Namecheap and Route 53 create a split-control model for domain ownership versus DNS changes. That is workable, but only if renewal, DNS access, and email-auth records are tightly managed.

  • Confirm ownership and recovery access for both registrar and DNS provider
  • Enforce SPF, DKIM, and DMARC policy with documented change control
  • Limit who can change DNS and mailbox routing

Google Workspace + Drive needs a formal storage model Medium

Google Workspace is a good operational backbone, but small teams often blur the line between personal My Drive storage, shared folders, and durable team-owned files.

  • Define a single shared-drive structure for listings, transactions, and brand assets
  • Move critical files out of individual user accounts where needed
  • Apply external sharing rules for contracts, wire-sensitive documents, and templates

Lofty, DocuSign, and QuickBooks likely create manual handoffs Medium

The workflow is strong conceptually, but there is likely still manual re-keying when a deal moves from lead management to contracts to accounting.

  • Map exactly when a lead becomes a transaction record
  • Document what fields get copied into DocuSign and QuickBooks
  • Remove duplicate entry where integration or standard forms can replace it

Canva output and social distribution need ownership rules Low

Canva is ideal for fast listing and social content, but teams often lose track of final assets, brand templates, and who can publish what.

  • Store approved exports in Google Drive, not only inside personal Canva workspaces
  • Define approved templates and naming standards
  • Align social channels to a single asset library and posting checklist

Prioritized Action Plan

1

Lock down domain, DNS, and email authentication

Confirm registrar ownership in Namecheap, DNS ownership in AWS Route 53, and publish a clean, documented email-auth posture for Google Workspace.

Target outcome: reduced impersonation and wire-fraud exposure.

Impact: High
Effort: Low–Medium
Suggested window: Week 1
2

Make Lofty the source of truth for active leads

Define exactly what enters Lofty from Google lead sources, who owns response SLAs, and where data should stop being re-entered in side spreadsheets or emails.

Target outcome: faster response and cleaner lead visibility.

Impact: High
Effort: Medium
Suggested window: Weeks 1–2
3

Standardize Google Drive and Canva asset storage

Set folder ownership, naming conventions, and final-file publishing rules so listing materials, brand assets, and client docs are easy for the whole team to find.

Target outcome: less search time and less dependency on one person.

Impact: Medium
Effort: Medium
Suggested window: Weeks 2–3
4

Document the DocuSign to QuickBooks handoff

Clarify what contract and close-out data moves into back-office accounting, and reduce manual copy steps wherever possible.

Target outcome: smoother close-outs and fewer accounting misses.

Impact: Medium
Effort: Medium
Suggested window: Weeks 3–4
